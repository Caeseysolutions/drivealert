The P-DISC NO3 — officially approved

The OOONO P-DISC NO3 is the only electronic parking disc with official E1 type approval from Germany's Kraftfahrt-Bundesamt (KBA). This is the same approval body that certifies all road vehicles in Germany. The E1 approval means the P-DISC NO3 is legally recognised as a valid parking disc — the time it displays is legally valid in blue parking zones across Germany.

🇩🇪 E1 approval — Kraftfahrt-Bundesamt (KBA) — Germany's Federal Motor Transport Authority. The P-DISC NO3 carries this official approval, making it legally equivalent to a paper parking disc in all German blue zones.

If you park in German blue zones regularly — whether you live there, commute, or travel frequently — the P-DISC NO3 eliminates the need to manually set a paper disc every time you park. It detects when your vehicle stops and sets the arrival time automatically. Solar powered. No batteries, no app, no subscription.

The CO-DRIVER NO2 — driver's responsibility

The situation for the CO-DRIVER NO2 in Germany is different. German road traffic law StVO §23 places responsibility on the driver for any device that could distract from safe driving. OOONO sells the CO-DRIVER on Amazon.de and includes documentation explaining this legal framework.

StVO §23 — German Road Traffic Regulations

The driver is responsible for ensuring that their view and hearing are not impaired, and that they are not distracted from traffic. Devices that warn of speed cameras are used at the driver's own responsibility. OOONO sells the CO-DRIVER in Germany with this note included.

In practice, millions of German drivers use the CO-DRIVER NO2 daily. The device is available on Amazon.de and OOONO actively markets it in Germany. The key distinction is that Germany has not banned these devices — it has simply made their use the driver's personal responsibility.
